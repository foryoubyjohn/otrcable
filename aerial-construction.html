What Aerial Construction Means

When cable providers, telecom companies, and municipalities need coaxial or fiber optic cable strung on utility poles, they call crews like ours. We work in the communications space — below the electric lines — lashing cable to strand, making attachments at the pole, and building out the backbone of broadband and cable TV networks.

Our linemen climb poles and operate bucket trucks to do this work safely and efficiently. Where the cable needs to transition underground — down a riser on a pole and into a conduit — we handle that too. We're not just installers; we're communications linemen who understand the full aerial plant from end to end.

This is a skilled trade, and we take pride in doing it right — clean lashing, proper clearances, solid attachments, and work that passes inspection the first time.

What We Build & Install

Coaxial Cable — Aerial Strand & Lash

We install coax cable on utility poles for cable TV and broadband distribution. Cable is lashed to a steel messenger strand that carries the load, keeping everything tight and compliant.

Fiber Optic — Aerial Installation

Single-mode fiber for trunk and feeder routes, lashed or self-supporting (ADSS). We handle splicing, closures, and terminations to deliver a complete, tested aerial fiber system.

Pole Attachment & Make-Ready

New cable starts with proper attachment to the pole. We transfer and rearrange existing cables to create clearance (make-ready work) and install new hardware so attachments are code-compliant.

Underground Transitions (Risers)

Where aerial cable transitions underground, we run riser conduit down the pole and coordinate the burial. Aerial and underground — we handle both sides of the transition.

Service Drops

From the distribution cable on the pole to the customer's home or business — we install aerial drops, including coax and fiber, to complete the last-mile connection.

How Our Crews Operate

Aerial construction is technical, physical, and safety-critical. Here's what it looks like when OTR is on the job.

Licensed Linemen

Our crews are trained and experienced communications linemen — people who climb poles and operate aerial equipment for a living. No corners cut on safety or technique.

Bucket Trucks & Climb Gear

We run properly equipped bucket trucks for production aerial work and use gaffs and climbing gear where trucks can't reach. The right tool for the terrain.

Clean Work That Passes

We meet NESC clearance requirements, follow proper lashing intervals, and keep the plant looking professional. Our work passes utility and customer inspections.
