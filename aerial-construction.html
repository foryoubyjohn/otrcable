The Work

Cable on Poles, Done Right

Our bread and butter is aerial construction — stringing coaxial and fiber optic cable on utility poles below the electric lines. This is skilled trade work that most contractors can't do and shouldn't attempt.

Our crews climb poles and operate bucket trucks to lash cable, make pole attachments, install hardware, and handle the underground transition where cable comes off the pole and goes into conduit. If you need cable on poles done right — clean lashing, proper clearances, work that passes inspection — you need a crew that does this for a living.

  • Coaxial Aerial Strand & Lash — Installation for cable TV and broadband distribution networks
  • Aerial Fiber Optic — Single-mode fiber with splicing, closures, and full termination
  • Pole Attachment & Make-Ready — Proper attachment work below the electric lines
  • Underground Risers — Transition from aerial to underground conduit
  • Service Drops — Last-mile connections from the distribution plant to the customer
  • Pole Climbing & Bucket Truck — Licensed crews with proper equipment and certifications
How We Work

Our Crews in the Field

Every aerial job is staffed with experienced linemen who know the trade inside and out.

Licensed Linemen

Our crews are trained, certified communications linemen — not general laborers learning on the job. They know pole work, clearance requirements, and NESC standards.

Bucket Trucks & Climb Gear

We own and maintain our own bucket trucks and climbing equipment. No renting, no waiting — we show up ready to work on day one.

Inspection-Ready Work

Clean lashing, proper sag, correct clearances, documented attachments. Our work passes inspection the first time because we do it right the first time.
